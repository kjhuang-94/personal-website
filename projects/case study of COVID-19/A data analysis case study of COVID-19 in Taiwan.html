A data analysis case study of COVID-19 in Taiwan¶

Author: Kuan-ju Huang
Data up to 2020/06/01

1. Introduction¶

On March 11, 2020, the World Health Organization (WHO) declared novel coronavirus (COVID-19) as a global pandemic. Until now, there are more than 7,000,000 infected cases and more than 430,000 deaths around the world. Although not being part of the WHO, Taiwan reported the first confirmed infected case on January 21, 2020, and started taking a series of precaution to prevent the virus from spreading out. Six months later (June 01, 2020),Taiwan has 443 infected cases, 7 deaths and 431 recovery cases. In this project, the data analysis of COVID-19 is based on a case study of Taiwan. There are three sections in the project,

  1. A brief overview of the COVID-19 in Taiwan and its trend
  2. Distribution of the COVID-19 confirmed cases based on different categories
  3. The relations between total confirmed cases, deaths and recovery cases

def reconstruct(s1, s2, t1, t2):
    for i in range(t1.shape[0]):
        for j in range(t2.shape[0]):
            if t1.loc[i,'Date'] == t2.iloc[j][s1]:
                t1.loc[i,s2]+=1
          
    return t1

                                

Covid19_Taiwan_cases = reconstruct('', 'Domestic', Covid19_Taiwan_cases, Covid19_cases_domestic)
Covid19_Taiwan_cases = reconstruct('', 'Imported', Covid19_Taiwan_cases, Covid19_cases_imported)  
Covid19_Taiwan_cases = reconstruct('', 'Death', Covid19_Taiwan_cases, Covid19_death)

  4. After WHO declared COVID-19 as a global pandemic, the daily imported confirmed cases increased rapidly and reached the peak on March 20. Later on, the daily imported confirmed cases started to decrease gradually.

  5. The increasing rate of total confirmed cases is relatively faster than the total deaths.

5. Conclusions¶

In this project, the situation of COVID-19 in Taiwan has been examined in different aspects, including the current trend, distribution of confirmed cases and relations between variables. The conclusions are as follows:

  1. The amount of confirmed cases is still increasing but the increasing rate is relatively slow compared with three month before.
  2. The total of deaths is less than 1.5% of the total of confirmed cases.
  3. More than 80% of the confirmed cases are imported cases and most of the confirmed cases are in the north part of Taiwan, especially the capital, Taipei City.
  4. Currently, the amount of recovery cases is increasing stably and faster than the confirmed cases and deaths.
